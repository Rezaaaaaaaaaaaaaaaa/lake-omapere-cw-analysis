   BaselineTP = TPAgGen + soilP + TPGen

   CW_Reduction = WetlandTP × CW_Coverage% × LRF
   Final_with_CW = WetlandTP - CW_Reduction

================================================================================
4. ROUTED LOADS CALCULATION
================================================================================

This follows your Pokaiwhenua DNZ approach and accounts for network effects.

METHODOLOGY:

For each reach, calculate in HYDSEQ order (upstream → downstream):

   Routed[i] = Generated[i] + Σ(Upstream_Routed[j] × PstreamCarry[i])

Where:
   - Generated[i] = Local TP from reach i
   - Upstream_Routed[j] = Already-routed load from immediate upstream reach
   - PstreamCarry[i] = Attenuation factor (TP retention during transport)

PROCESS:
   1. Organize 50 Lake reaches + upstream contributors by HYDSEQ
   2. Calculate Routed_Baseline for all reaches
   3. Calculate Routed_Wetland for all reaches
   4. Calculate Routed_CW for all reaches
   5. Compute reductions: Baseline - CW for each reach

RESULTS (Routed Loads for Lake Ōmāpere):
   Baseline Routed TP:      2.210 tpy
   Wetland Only:            2.270 tpy
   Wetland + CW:            2.041 tpy
   ──────────────────────────────────
   CW Reduction:            0.229 tpy (7.7% improvement)

   KEY FINDING: 8× LARGER THAN GENERATED (0.229 vs 0.029 tpy)

================================================================================
5. WHY ROUTING SHOWS MUCH LARGER BENEFITS
================================================================================

1. UPSTREAM AMPLIFICATION
   Baseline routed (2.210 tpy) vs generated (0.297 tpy) = 7.4× difference
   This shows cumulative effects of all upstream reaches flowing to Lake

2. CW BENEFIT PROPAGATION
   - CW in upstream reaches reduces local loads
   - These reductions propagate downstream through network
   - Each downstream reach benefits from upstream improvements

3. NETWORK EFFECTS EXAMPLE
   - 24 HighClay reaches (no CW): Still show 0.057 tpy routed reduction
   - Why? Benefits from upstream CW implementation!
   - 17 NoCW reaches: Show 0.140 tpy routed reduction
   - Same reason: upstream CW feeds downstream benefit

This is why routing is more realistic for predicting Lake water quality impacts.
